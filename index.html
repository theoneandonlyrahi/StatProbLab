Welcome to StatProbLab v.1

An interactive platform for learning probability and statistics. Explore data analysis, probability distributions, regression, and simulation through a modern, accessible interface.

Developed by: Rahidul Alam

StatProbLab is designed to make learning statistics and probability more engaging, interactive, and easier to understand. Whether you are a student learning the basics for the first time or an educator searching for helpful teaching tools, StatProbLab offers a variety of features that support learning through exploration and hands-on practice.

I created StatProbLab to provide a clean and organized space where users can explore statistical and probabilistic concepts in a simple and approachable way. The goal was to build an interactive learning environment that helps users better visualize and understand important ideas while offering an alternative to Stapplet, a program commonly used in many school districts.

To access the different features and tools available in StatProbLab, use the sidebar located on the left side of the screen to navigate throughout the website.

Core Features

  • Categorical Data Analysis: bar charts, segmented bar charts, pie charts, and stacked bar charts
  • Quantitative Data Analysis: histograms, box plots, and scatter plots
  • Distributions: normal, binomial, and other common distributions
  • Simulations: Monte Carlo simulations and other probabilistic models

Categorical Data Analysis

Analyze and display categorical data through various visualizations such as bar charts, segmented bar charts, pie charts, and stacked bar charts.

Categorical Data Analysis is the process of inputting, examining, and analyzing information that is grouped into categories rather than measured numerically. These categories can include things such as favorite colors, types of pets, customer satisfaction levels, political preferences, or product choices. Instead of focusing on calculations like averages, categorical data analysis examines comparisons, proportions, frequencies, and relationships between groups. This type of analysis helps organize and interpret non-numerical information, making it easier to identify patterns and understand how different groups relate to one another.

Quantitative Data Analysis

Analyze and display quantitative data through various visualizations such as histograms, box plots, and scatter plots.

Quantitative Data Analysis is the process of inputting, examining, and analyzing information that is measured using numbers rather than categories. This type of data can include values such as height, weight, temperature, income, ages, or test scores. Quantitative analysis focuses on identifying patterns, averages, differences, trends, and relationships within numerical data. By using mathematical and statistical methods, it helps researchers and analysts draw conclusions, make predictions, and better understand measurable information in a clear and organized way.

Distributions

Analyze and display probability distributions to model real-world events through continuous and discrete models.

Distributions are mathematical models used to show how likely different outcomes are in a random event or experiment. They help explain how data is spread out and which results are more common or less common. By using distributions, we can better understand patterns, predict possible outcomes, and make sense of uncertainty in real-world situations. They are widely used in statistics, science, business, and data analysis to organize information and visualize how values or events are expected to occur over time or across different conditions.

Simulations

Explore various interesting simulation scenarios such as Monte Carlo methods, random walks, and dice rolls through interactive input.

Simulations allow users to explore probability and statistics by modeling real-world random events in an interactive way. These activities help demonstrate how chance, variation, and probability work through repeated trials and visual results. By running simulations, users can observe patterns, compare outcomes, and better understand important statistical concepts through hands-on experimentation. StatProbLab includes a variety of simulations, such as coin flips, dice rolls, random walks, card draws, and probability experiments, all designed to make learning more engaging and intuitive.
