Welcome to StatProbLab v.1

An interactive platform for learning probability and statistics. Explore data analysis, probability distributions, regression, and simulation through a modern, accessible interface.

Developed by: Rahidul Alam

StatProbLab is designed to make learning statistics and probability engaging and intuitive. Whether you're a student new to the subject or an educator looking for interactive teaching tools, StatProbLab offers a variety of features to enhance your understanding of key concepts.

I built StatProbLab because I wanted to create a single, clean place to explore and understand statistical or probabilistic concepts. StatProbLab provides as an alternative interactive learning environment to Stapplet, an app widely used by other school districts.

A huge thank you to my Probability and Statistics teacher, Ms. Lin, for making this subject exciting and engaging. Your way of teaching through simulations and scenarios is a big reason this project exists!

In order to access the features below, you need to navigate through the sidebar on the left.

Core Features

  • Categorical Data Analysis: bar charts, segmented bar charts, pie charts, and stacked bar charts
  • Quantitative Data Analysis: histograms, box plots, and scatter plots
  • Distributions: normal, binomial, and other common distributions
  • Simulations: Monte Carlo simulations and other probabilistic models

Categorical Data Analysis

Analyze and display categorical data through various visualizations such as bar charts, segmented bar charts, pie charts, and stacked bar charts.

One Variable Single Group

Analyze and display single-variable categorical data through bar charts, segmented bar charts, and pie charts.

One Variable Multiple Groups

Analyze and display multiple-variable categorical data through grouped bar charts and stacked bar charts.

Two Variable Categorical Data

Analyze and display two-variable categorical data through side-by-side charts, segmented bar charts, and mosaic plots.

Quantitative Data Analysis

Analyze and display quantitative data through various visualizations such as histograms, box plots, and scatter plots.

One Variable Single Group

Analyze and display single-variable quantitative data through histograms, box plots, and scatter plots.

One Variable Multiple Groups

Analyze and display multiple-variable quantitative data through histograms, box plots, and scatter plots.

Two Variable Quantitative Data

Analyze and display two-variable quantitative data through scatter plots.

Distributions

Analyze and display probability distributions to model real-world events through continuous and discrete models.

Normal Distribution

Explore the properties of the normal distribution to model real-world data.

Uniform Distribution

Explore the properties of the uniform distribution to model equally likely outcomes.

Geometric Distribution

Explore the properties of the geometric distribution to model the number of trials until the first success.

Binomial Distribution

Explore the properties of the binomial distribution to model the number of successes in a fixed number of trials.

Poisson Distribution

Explore the properties of the poisson distribution to model the number of events in a fixed interval.

Simulations

Explore various interesting simulation scenarios such as Monte Carlo methods, random walks, and dice rolls through interactive input.

Monte Carlo Pi Simulation

Simulate the estimation of pi using the famous Monte Carlo method.

Dice Roll Simulation

Simulate rolling dice and analyze the distribution of outcomes.

Random Walk

Simulate a random walk with equal probabilities and analyze the resulting paths.

Coin Flip

Simulate flipping a coin and analyze the distribution of outcomes.

Spinner

Simulate a spinner with equal probability sections and analyze the distribution of outcomes.

Card Draw

Simulate drawing cards from a deck and analyze the distribution of outcome with or without replacement.

Same Birthday

Simulate the probability of two people in a group having the same birthday.
